People's Power

Overview

The People Power Revolution, also known as the EDSA Revolution, was a peaceful uprising in the Philippines from February 22 to 25, 1986. It led to the overthrow of Ferdinand Marcos and the restoration of democracy under Corazon Aquino. Millions of Filipinos protested, demonstrating the power of unity and nonviolent resistance.
